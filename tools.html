How to actually improve your SAT score
  • Take one full timed practice test before you study anything else — you need a real baseline, not a guess.
  • Drill your weakest section daily in short 20–30 minute blocks rather than cramming one long session weekly.
  • For Math, memorise the formulas you keep forgetting mid-test — a small flashcard deck beats re-deriving them under time pressure.
  • For Reading/Writing, read the question before the passage so you know what to scan for.
  • Re-take full practice tests every 2–3 weeks to track real progress, not just isolated drills.
How to actually improve your IELTS score
  • Practice under real time pressure — most band loss comes from running out of time, not from not knowing the answer.
  • Build topic-based vocabulary (education, environment, technology, health) since these recur across Speaking and Writing.
  • Record yourself answering Speaking Part 2 questions and listen back — most fluency issues are audible only on playback.
  • For Writing Task 2, memorise a flexible essay structure (intro → two developed points → conclusion) rather than improvising each time.
  • Check whether your target universities accept Duolingo English Test instead — it's faster and cheaper, but not all programs accept it. See the checker below.
